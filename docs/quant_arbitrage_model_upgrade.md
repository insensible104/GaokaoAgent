# Quant Arbitrage Model Upgrade

Date: 2026-05-21

This upgrade turns the recent "volunteer arbitrage" discussion into a first concrete quantitative model. It does not replace the existing admission-probability model. It adds a separate layer for personalized opportunity discovery.

## Why Monte Carlo Is Not The Core

The existing Monte Carlo module samples possible cutoff ranks from historical rank volatility. That is useful for uncertainty display, confidence intervals, and first-hit portfolio risk.

But it does not directly model the real arbitrage mechanism:

```text
market discount source
+ student-specific tolerance
+ relative tier lift
+ within-group front-major assignment
+ rebound risk from publicity
```

So Monte Carlo remains an uncertainty tool, not the main opportunity-discovery engine.

## Model Objects

### `StudentValueModel`

Represents family/student weights and tolerances:

- brand / face value
- employment ROI weight
- public-school preference
- cost sensitivity
- major strictness
- region, tuition, campus, pathway, adjustment, employment-uncertainty tolerance
- regret sensitivity

### `CounterfactualBaseline`

Represents the same-rank conventional outcome:

- school tier
- public/private status
- major quality
- city tier
- cost type

This gives the system a denominator for "leak" or "tier lift."

### `SacrificeVector`

Represents what the candidate requires the student to give up:

- region
- major
- tuition
- campus
- pathway
- adjustment
- employment uncertainty

### `OpportunitySignals`

Represents why the market might be underpricing the group:

- cold-major discount
- tuition filter
- campus discount
- region discount
- new-major uncertainty
- group restructure
- historical-anchor overdeterrence
- standalone-major anchor discount
- low-attention signal
- sentiment shock discount
- quota expansion
- evidence strength
- rebound risk

### `AdmissionContext`

Represents group admission and within-group assignment:

- group admission probability
- front-major quota share
- entrant-pool discount
- historical-anchor overdeterrence
- major obvious heat
- rank margin
- tail-assignment risk
- front-major hit probability

It includes `from_major_options(...)` to estimate front-major quota share and tail risk from structured major options.

## Core Score

The model computes:

```text
ArbitrageScore =
  relative_lift
  + market_discount_score
  + personal_acceptability
  + admission_feasibility
  + assignment_opportunity
  - sacrifice_cost
  - tail_assignment_risk
  - rebound_risk
```

For Dalian-like cases, it also computes:

```text
FrontMajorArbitrageScore =
  P(group_admitted)
  * P(front_major_hit | group_admitted)
  * front_major_value
  * relative_lift
  * personal_acceptability
  * (1 - rebound_risk)
  * (1 - tail_risk_penalty)
```

## What The Tests Lock Down

`test_arbitrage_score_rewards_affordable_brand_discount`

- The same high-tuition/campus-discount opportunity scores much higher for a rich, brand-seeking family than for a cost-sensitive employment-ROI family.
- This checks that "opportunity" is conditional on the student, not universal.

`test_front_major_probability_penalizes_hot_tiny_quota_major`

- A front major with reasonable quota and lower heat gets a higher front-major hit probability.
- A hot major with tiny quota and bad tail options is penalized.

`test_arbitrage_result_survives_volunteer_plan_conversion`

- The arbitrage result can be attached to a `MajorGroupRow`.
- The result survives conversion into `VolunteerChoice` and `VolunteerPlan`.

`test_adapter_scores_existing_major_group_row`

- Existing recommendation rows can be scored without manually constructing all model inputs.
- The adapter writes `arbitrage_score`, `front_major_hit_prob`, `opportunity_types`, and `opportunity_pools` back to the row.

## Current Integration Status

Every row generated by `game_agent.py` now receives an arbitrage score after the regular tradeoff score is computed.

The current adapter derives transparent inputs from existing row/profile data:

- same-rank baseline from rank band
- student tolerance from school/major preference, risk tolerance, and cost concerns
- sacrifice vector from tuition, campus labels, cold-major mix, city mismatch, and tail risk
- market-discount signals from tuition, campus, major mix, quota variance, and historical-anchor proxies
- front-major hit probability from in-group major quota, preferred major keywords, entrant-pool discount, heat, and tail risk

Ablation v2 added three signal-family probes:

| Variant | Success | Preferred Major | Tail Assignment | Avg Utility | Interpretation |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| full | 64.4% | 13.7% | 26.0% | 0.468 | Current production-style policy. |

The important research update is that the broad 73-case run now identifies a sharper target:

```text
The next policy should not simply maximize "arbitrage score."
It should optimize front-major hit under a group-admission and tail-risk constraint.
```

In other words, the Dalian-like opportunity should be modeled as a constrained two-stage objective:

```text
maximize    P(group_admitted) * P(front_major_hit | group_admitted) * utility(front_major)
subject to  sliding risk <= budget
            tail assignment risk <= budget
            sacrifice cost <= student tolerance
```

Opportunity coverage in selected choices:

```text
plan_choices: 669
front_major_arbitrage_pool: 200
relative_tier_lift_pool: 9
rush_opportunity_pool: 326
safe_anchor_pool: 287
avg_arbitrage_score: 0.4026
avg_front_major_hit_prob: 0.6327
```

Interpretation:

- Larger-case coverage is now much better than the earlier 12-case run.
- The current arbitrage layer is present in candidate scoring and selection.
- A generic arbitrage score is not enough; `arbitrage_only` increases tail risk.
- `front_major_boost` is the strongest current probe, raising preferred-major hit from 13.7% to 28.8% with the same 64.4% success rate.
- The next modeling target should be a constrained within-group assignment objective, not more Monte Carlo sampling.

## 2026-05-21 Guarded Runtime Experiment

Added a guarded policy probe:

- `guarded_arbitrage` ablation variant
- stronger runtime scoring in `backend/src/rl/runtime_policy.py`
- tests in `backend/src/test_market_simulation_smoke.py`

The core rule is:

```text
Do not promote an opportunity just because segment demand or arbitrage score is high.
Promote it only when admission feasibility, major utility, tail risk, and rebound risk pass guards.
```

New runtime score terms:

- positive:
  - `arbitrage_score`
  - `front_major_arbitrage_score`
  - `front_major_hit_prob`
  - `segment_demand_score`
  - `low_attention_signal`
- negative:
  - `tail_assignment_risk`
  - `segment_rebound_risk`
  - `rebound_risk`
  - blacklist risk

Small 29-case backtest on `logs/frozen_plans_2025_guarded_runtime_v2.jsonl`:

```text
success_rate: 72.4%
preferred_major_hit_rate: 17.2%
tail_assignment_rate: 34.5%
average_assigned_major_utility: 0.506
average_first_hit_index: 1.81
```

Compared with the previous segment-market frozen run, the main quality metrics
did not move, while average first-hit index improved slightly. This is useful
negative evidence:

```text
The signal is present, but production full ranking is not yet bottlenecked by
single-row scoring. The next upgrade should change portfolio construction and
first-hit-aware ordering, not only row-level weights.
```

Current best ablation probes on the same 29-case set:

| Variant | Success | Preferred Major | Tail Assignment | Avg Utility |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| full | 72.4% | 17.2% | 34.5% | 0.506 |
| arbitrage_only | 72.4% | 20.7% | 20.7% | 0.510 |
| segment_market | 72.4% | 17.2% | 20.7% | 0.512 |
| guarded_arbitrage | 72.4% | 17.2% | 31.0% | 0.517 |

Research interpretation:

- `arbitrage_only` is better for preferred-major hit on this small sample.
- `segment_market` is better for tail-risk control.
- `guarded_arbitrage` is better for average utility, but not yet for preferred-major hit.
- The next policy should optimize the ordered volunteer prefix directly:

```text
maximize preferred/front-major hit and utility
subject to success probability, tail-risk, blacklist, and rebound-risk budgets
```

## 2026-05-23 First-Hit Prefix Optimizer

Added a direct ordered-prefix optimizer:

- `backend/src/recommendation/prefix_optimizer.py`
- `backend/src/test_prefix_optimizer_smoke.py`
- `build_volunteer_plan(..., optimize_prefix=True)`
- `game_agent.py` production path now enables the prefix optimizer

The goal is to model the key volunteer-form mechanism explicitly:

```text
The first admitted group shadows every later group.
So the ranking problem is not just row scoring.
It is a constrained prefix ordering problem.
```

The optimizer separates:

- reliable value anchors: high-admission, low-tail rows with acceptable utility
- opportunity rows: target/rush rows with acceptable tail and rebound risk
- safe anchors: rows explicitly tagged `SAFE`
- reckless rows: blacklist, excessive tail risk, or excessive rebound risk

Then it orders the prefix as:

```text
reliable value anchors
+ clean high-value opportunity rows
+ reserved safe anchors
+ tail-heavy opportunity rows
+ remaining safe rows
+ reckless rows
```

This is wired as a portfolio post-processor rather than a replacement for the
row-level recommender:

```text
candidate generation
-> row scoring / arbitrage / segment demand / runtime policy
-> prefix optimizer
-> first-hit metrics
-> report / critic / supervisor review
```

Regression fixed during this upgrade:

```text
Do not reclassify a `TARGET` row as a safe anchor merely because its predicted
admission probability is high. A high-probability target can still be the exact
"borderline leak" that should stay in the opportunity prefix.
```

This came from the `历史_rank_078000` backtest case. Before the fix, a real
广东培正学院 target row was pushed out of the top 10 by high-probability safe
rows. After the fix, success is preserved.

Tail-risk upgrade:

```text
Do not let ordinary rush rows automatically shadow reliable value anchors.
A reliable anchor is high-probability, low-tail, non-blacklist, and has
acceptable utility / market evidence. Tail-heavy opportunities wait behind
reserved anchors unless they have standout front-major value.
```

Clean 29-case 2025 ablation on:

```text
logs/frozen_plans_2025_prefix_clean.jsonl
```

| Variant | Success | Preferred Major | Tail Assignment | Avg Utility | Avg First-Hit Index |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| full | 72.4% | 17.2% | 34.5% | 0.506 | 1.81 |
| arbitrage_only | 72.4% | 20.7% | 20.7% | 0.510 | 2.00 |
| segment_market | 72.4% | 17.2% | 20.7% | 0.512 | 2.10 |
| guarded_arbitrage | 72.4% | 17.2% | 31.0% | 0.517 | 2.29 |
| prefix_optimizer | 72.4% | 20.7% | 20.7% | 0.529 | 1.62 |

Interpretation:

- `prefix_optimizer` is the current best small-sample probe for average utility.
- It improves preferred-major hit without sacrificing group success.
- After reliable-anchor and tail-heavy guards, it matches the best tail-control
  probes while keeping a better average utility and earlier first-hit index.
- The next research step is a joint optimizer that combines prefix ordering with
  within-group major-name normalization, because several "tail" labels come
  from selected-major name mismatch rather than truly bad in-group assignment.
